GSA Schedule Certification

What is a GSA Schedule? A GSA Schedule (also called Multiple Award Schedule or Federal Supply Schedule) is a long-term governmentwide contract with commercial companies that provide access to millions of federal, state, and local buyers.

Why Get a GSA Schedule?

Increased Revenue

Access to $50+ billion in annual government spending

Simplified Procurement

Pre-negotiated pricing speeds up buying process

Nationwide Access

Sell to any federal agency across the country

GSA Schedule Categories for Cleaning Services

  • Schedule 03FAC: Facilities Maintenance and Management Most Common for Cleaning
  • Special Item Number (SIN) 561210: Facilities Support Services
  • SIN 561720: Janitorial Services
  • SIN 561790: Other Services to Buildings and Dwellings

Getting Started with GSA

  • Be in business for at least 2 years
  • Have a DUNS number and SAM.gov registration
  • Have commercial sales history
  • Be financially stable
  • Comply with labor laws and regulations

  • Review GSA's website and resources
  • Identify the right schedule and SIN
  • Gather required documentation
  • Understand pricing requirements
  • Consider hiring a GSA consultant

  • Complete the offer online via eOffer
  • Provide detailed pricing information
  • Submit financial statements
  • Include past performance references
  • Typical review time: 90-120 days

Best Practices for Government Contracting Success

Before You Start
  • Register in SAM.gov (required for all federal contracts)
  • Get a DUNS/UEI number
  • Set up accounting system for government contracts
  • Ensure you have adequate bonding capacity
  • Build a strong past performance record
  • Create capability statement
Finding Opportunities
  • Monitor SAM.gov and beta.SAM.gov daily
  • Set up automated email alerts
  • Attend pre-bid conferences
  • Build relationships with contracting officers
  • Join industry associations (ISSA, BSCAI)
  • Network at matchmaking events
Writing Winning Proposals
  • Read the entire solicitation carefully
  • Answer every requirement explicitly
  • Use the same language as the RFP
  • Highlight your unique qualifications
  • Provide specific examples and metrics
  • Have someone proofread before submission
  • Submit well before deadline
Growing Your Business
  • Start with smaller contracts (under $250K)
  • Build strong past performance record
  • Ask for debriefs after losses
  • Consider subcontracting first
  • Maintain excellent contract performance
  • Get testimonials from contracting officers
  • Scale gradually as you gain experience
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Not reading the entire solicitation
  • Missing submission deadlines
  • Incomplete or incorrect forms
  • Unrealistic pricing
  • Weak past performance section
  • Poor quality control procedures
  • Inadequate staffing plans
  • Not following up after submission
  • Ignoring small business set-asides
  • Failing to maintain SAM.gov registration
