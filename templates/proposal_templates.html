Executive Summary Template

How to Write a Compelling Executive Summary

The executive summary is the first (and sometimes only) section evaluators read. Make it count!

Template Structure:

[Your Company Name] - Executive Summary

Project: [RFP Title and Number]

1. Understanding (2-3 sentences)
Example: We understand that [Agency Name] requires comprehensive janitorial services for [Building/Facility] totaling approximately [X] square feet. The primary objectives include maintaining high cleanliness standards, ensuring safety compliance, and providing environmentally responsible cleaning solutions.

2. Your Solution (2-3 sentences)
Example: [Your Company] proposes a customized cleaning program utilizing our proven 5-step quality assurance process. Our team of [X] certified cleaning professionals will deliver consistent, high-quality service using EPA-approved green cleaning products and state-of-the-art equipment.

3. Why Choose Us (3-4 bullet points)

  • [X] years serving government facilities in Virginia
  • Current holder of [relevant contract or certification]
  • Zero safety incidents in the past [X] years
  • CIMS-GB certified with a 98% quality score average

4. Key Personnel
Our project team is led by [Name], [Title], who has [X] years of experience managing government cleaning contracts exceeding $[X]M annually.

5. Competitive Advantage (1-2 sentences)
Example: Our competitive pricing of $[X] per square foot represents a [X]% cost savings while maintaining superior quality standards. We guarantee 24/7 response time for urgent requests and provide real-time digital inspection reports.

Pro Tips:
  • Keep it to 1-2 pages maximum
  • Use specific numbers and data
  • Address the top 3 evaluation criteria
  • Write it LAST, after completing other sections

Technical Approach Guide

Structuring Your Technical Approach

Recommended Sections:
1. Scope of Work Understanding

Demonstrate you understand ALL requirements by listing them:

  • Daily cleaning tasks (restrooms, offices, common areas)
  • Weekly/monthly deep cleaning
  • Special services (carpet cleaning, window washing, etc.)
  • Coverage hours and staffing requirements
2. Cleaning Methodology

Describe your approach:

  • Inspection Process: Daily checklists, weekly audits, monthly reviews
  • Quality Control: Supervisor spot-checks, customer feedback system
  • Green Cleaning: EPA-approved products, microfiber systems
  • Technology: Digital work orders, real-time reporting
3. Staffing Plan

Include a table showing:

Position Number Hours/Week Qualifications
Site Supervisor 1 40 5+ years, CIMS certified
Team Leaders 2 40 each 2+ years, first aid certified
Cleaners 8 40 each Background checked, trained
4. Safety & Training Program
  • New employee orientation (40 hours)
  • OSHA safety training
  • Monthly refresher training
  • Bloodborne pathogen certification
5. Implementation Timeline
Week Activity
1 Mobilization, staff hiring, equipment procurement
2 Staff training, site walkthroughs
3 Begin operations with enhanced oversight
4 Full operations, first quality audit

Past Performance Template

How to Present Past Performance

Template for Each Project:
Project #1: [Client Name]

Contract Value: $[X] annually

Period of Performance: [Start Date] - [End Date or "Present"]

Square Footage: [X] sq ft

Facility Type: [Office/School/Medical/etc.]

Staff Size: [X] FTE employees

Services: Daily janitorial, floor care, etc.

Description: Provide daily janitorial services for a [X] square foot [type] facility including [list main tasks]. Our team of [X] cleaners maintains high standards across [X] buildings.

Relevance: This project demonstrates our capability to [explain how it's similar to the RFP requirements].

Performance Highlights:

  • Maintained 98% quality inspection scores
  • Zero safety incidents in [X] years
  • Completed [X] additional projects on time and under budget

Reference Contact:
[Name], [Title]
[Phone] | [Email]

Best Practices:
  • List 3-5 most relevant projects
  • Choose projects similar in size and scope to the RFP
  • Include current projects if performing well
  • Always get permission before listing references
  • Pre-brief references on what evaluators might ask
