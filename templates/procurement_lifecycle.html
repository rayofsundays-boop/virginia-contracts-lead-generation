Phase 1: Requirements Determination & Planning

FAR Reference: FAR Part 7 - Acquisition Planning
What Happens in This Phase:
  • Agency identifies a need for goods or services
  • Requirements are documented in a Statement of Work (SOW) or Performance Work Statement (PWS)
  • Market research is conducted to identify potential vendors
  • Budget is allocated and approved
  • Acquisition strategy is developed
  • Small business set-aside determinations are made
For Contractors:
  • Monitor agency forecasts and procurement schedules
  • Build relationships with contracting officers
  • Respond to Requests for Information (RFI)
  • Attend industry days and pre-solicitation conferences
Timeline:

Typically 2-6 months

Complex acquisitions may take longer

Phase 2: Solicitation

FAR Reference: FAR Part 15 - Contracting by Negotiation | FAR Part 14 - Sealed Bidding
Common Solicitation Types:
RFP - Request for Proposal

Use: Complex requirements, negotiated procurement

Award Basis: Best value (technical + price)

FAR: Part 15

IFB - Invitation for Bid

Use: Straightforward requirements

Award Basis: Lowest price

FAR: Part 14

RFQ - Request for Quotation

Use: Simplified acquisitions under $250K

Award Basis: Best value or lowest price

FAR: Part 13

Sources Sought Notice

Use: Market research, capability statements

Award Basis: Not a solicitation

FAR: Part 7

Where Solicitations Are Posted:
  • SAM.gov - System for Award Management (Federal) Visit
  • eVA - Virginia Procurement Portal (State) Visit
  • State & Local Portals Guide - Directory access View Guide
For Contractors:
  • Register in SAM.gov (required for federal contracts)
  • Set up email alerts for relevant opportunities
  • Read the entire solicitation carefully
  • Attend pre-proposal conferences if offered
  • Submit questions before the deadline
  • Start preparing your proposal immediately

Phase 3: Evaluation & Selection

FAR Reference: FAR 15.3 - Source Selection
Evaluation Process:
Step 1: Initial Review

Proposals checked for completeness and compliance with requirements

Step 2: Technical Evaluation

Technical panel scores proposals against evaluation criteria

Step 3: Best Value Decision

Contracting officer selects winner based on evaluation factors

Common Evaluation Criteria:
Factor Description Typical Weight
Technical Approach Understanding of requirements, methodology, quality control 30-40%
Past Performance Relevant experience, references, quality of prior work 20-30%
Management Key personnel qualifications, project management plan 15-25%
Price Cost competitiveness, price reasonableness 20-35%
Important: The lowest price does not always win! Agencies use "best value" selection, balancing technical merit and cost.

Phase 4: Contract Award

FAR Reference: FAR Part 4 - Administrative Matters | FAR 15.5 - Preaward, Award, and Postaward Notifications
Award Process Steps:
  1. Award Decision: Contracting Officer makes final selection
  2. Negotiations (if needed): Final pricing and terms discussions
  3. Award Notification: Winner notified, losers receive debriefings (if requested)
  4. Contract Signing: Both parties sign the contract
  5. Public Posting: Award posted on SAM.gov (federal) or agency website
  6. Protest Period: 10-day window for protests (federal contracts)
If You Win:
  • Review contract terms carefully
  • Clarify any questions before signing
  • Set up contract management system
  • Schedule kickoff meeting
  • Begin mobilization planning
If You Lose:
  • Request a debriefing (highly recommended)
  • Learn from feedback for next time
  • Consider protest only if there's a valid basis
  • Maintain professional relationships
  • Look for teaming opportunities

Phase 5: Contract Administration & Execution

FAR Reference: FAR Part 42 - Contract Administration | FAR Part 43 - Contract Modifications
Key Activities During Contract Performance:

  • Hold kickoff meeting with all stakeholders
  • Establish communication protocols
  • Confirm deliverable schedules and milestones
  • Set up invoicing and payment procedures
  • Assign Contracting Officer Representative (COR)

  • Regular status meetings and progress reports
  • Quality assurance surveillance plans (QASP)
  • Performance metrics tracking
  • Issue resolution and corrective actions
  • Document all communications

  • Submit invoices per contract terms (often monthly)
  • Include required backup documentation
  • Use proper invoice formats (WAWF for federal)
  • Track payment timelines (Prompt Payment Act - FAR 32.9)
  • Address any payment discrepancies immediately

  • Bilateral modifications: Both parties agree (most common)
  • Unilateral modifications: Government issues (administrative changes)
  • Document all scope changes in writing
  • Submit Request for Equitable Adjustment (REA) if costs increase
  • Maintain modification log
Critical: Never perform work outside the contract scope without a written modification! This protects both you and the government.

Phase 6: Contract Closeout

FAR Reference: FAR 4.804 - Closeout of Contract Files
Closeout Checklist:
  • All deliverables completed and accepted
  • Final invoices submitted and paid
  • Property/equipment returned (if applicable)
  • Release of claims submitted
  • Final performance ratings provided
  • Audit requirements satisfied
  • All modifications finalized
  • Records retention requirements understood
Building for Future Success:
  • Request letters of recommendation
  • Document lessons learned
  • Update past performance records
  • Maintain relationships for future opportunities
  • Retain contract files per FAR requirements (typically 3-6 years)

FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation) Reference Guide

What is the FAR?

The Federal Acquisition Regulation (FAR) is the primary regulation for all executive agencies in their acquisition of supplies and services with appropriated funds. It establishes uniform policies for federal procurement.

Full FAR: https://www.acquisition.gov/browse/index/far

Key FAR Parts for Contractors:
FAR Part Topic Why It Matters
Part 2 Definitions Understand key contracting terminology
Part 4 Administrative Requirements SAM.gov registration, CAGE codes, records retention
Part 7 Acquisition Planning How agencies plan procurements, market research
Part 9 Contractor Qualifications Responsibility standards, small business programs
Part 13 Simplified Acquisition Procedures Procurements under $250,000 (SAT)
Part 14 Sealed Bidding Formal advertising, IFBs, lowest price wins
Part 15 Contracting by Negotiation RFPs, source selection, best value
Part 19 Small Business Programs Set-asides, 8(a), HUBZone, WOSB, SDVOSB
Part 22 Labor Standards Davis-Bacon Act, Service Contract Act wages
Part 31 Contract Cost Principles Allowable/unallowable costs for cost-reimbursable contracts
Part 32 Contract Financing Progress payments, prompt payment (30 days)
Part 33 Protests, Disputes, Appeals How to challenge procurement decisions
Part 42 Contract Administration Performance monitoring, COR duties
Part 43 Contract Modifications How to change contract terms legally
Part 52 Contract Clauses Standard clauses incorporated in contracts
State & Local Regulations

While FAR governs federal contracts, states and localities have their own procurement rules:

  • Virginia: Virginia Public Procurement Act (VPPA)
  • Local: Each city/county has its own purchasing policies
  • Often mirror FAR principles but with variations
  • Check specific jurisdiction requirements

Contractor Success Tips

Registration & Credentials
  • Complete SAM.gov registration (federal)
  • Obtain necessary certifications (8(a), WOSB, etc.)
  • Maintain active licenses and insurance
  • Build past performance portfolio
Relationship Building
  • Attend industry days and networking events
  • Meet with agency small business liaisons
  • Join relevant industry associations
  • Build teaming relationships
Proposal Excellence
  • Follow instructions exactly
  • Address all evaluation criteria
  • Use active voice and clear language
  • Include compelling past performance examples
Pro Tip:

Start small! Many contractors begin with micro-purchases (under $10,000) or small set-asides to build past performance before pursuing larger contracts.
